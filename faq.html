What concession options are available at the theatre?

Standard cinema concessions include popcorn, candy, soft drinks, nachos, and hot dogs, with combo packages available at discounted rates.

The concession stand is a central part of the movie theatre experience, offering a variety of snacks and beverages to enjoy during your film. Popcorn remains the most popular choice and is typically available in multiple sizes, often with options for extra butter or seasoning. Candy selections usually include both classic movie theatre favorites and popular retail brands. When are matinee showtimes typically available?

Matinee showtimes are usually available during afternoon hours on weekdays and throughout the day on weekends, often at reduced ticket prices.

Matinee pricing represents one of the best opportunities to enjoy theatrical releases at a lower cost. The exact definition of "matinee" can vary by theatre, but it typically refers to showtimes before 5 PM or 6 PM on weekdays, with some theatres extending matinee pricing to all showtimes before a certain hour on weekends as well. The discount can be substantial, sometimes reducing ticket prices by several dollars compared to evening admission, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious moviegoers, students, seniors, and families.

Beyond the financial savings, matinee showtimes often provide a different atmosphere than evening screenings. Auditoriums tend to be less crowded during afternoon hours on weekdays, which can mean a quieter, more relaxed viewing experience with your choice of seats even if you arrive close to showtime. This makes matinees particularly appealing for families with young children who may need to leave if a child becomes restless, or for anyone who prefers a less crowded environment. Weekend matinees can be busier, especially for family-friendly films, but still generally offer more availability than prime-time evening slots.
